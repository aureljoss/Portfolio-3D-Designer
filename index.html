01. About me

Over the past ten years, I've built products for companies, businesses and government entities around the globe with a focus on design, usability, accessibility and inclusivity.

While the products I design have been in the built environment, the skills I have learned as an architect and designer are all applicable to the digital world. From working with multi-disciplinary teams, to iterating through design concepts and working with stakeholders, I am committed to meeting client expectations while creating great user experiences.

As I focus on my career transition to 3D experience designer, I have have also been immersed in the world of computer programming for more than a year with an emphasis on implementing modern web best practices. I am currently learning 3D web-based interactive rendering with Three.js and WebGL.

Tell me more!

Well since you've asked, I was born and raised in the south of France 🧀 (yes, I love cheese!). During my bachelors at the University of Clermont-Ferrand, I had the opportunity to study abroad in the US. One thing led to another, and here I am, ten years later, living and working in Washington D.C.

The stability provided from obtaining my green card a few years ago, allowed me to step back and reassess my passions and career goals. I came to realize that I found more value focussing my creative problem solving abilities on the rapidly evolving digital environment like the world of 3D experience design rather than building design. What used to be an itch to switch to a career in tech became a need.

In my downtime, you can find me eating tacos, biking, or at the dog park. I am also a bad but enthusiastic pickleball player who occasionally shows up at the D.C. queer pickleball league.
