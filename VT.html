The Process

The renderings and immersive scenes below were all created using the Revit model developed by the team for construction documentation. I then exported the model in CAD format to import into Sketchup. Revit models typically focus on the 2D documentation so a lot of the scene’s details still needed to be modeled in Sketchup. For interior scenes, materials were added to the Sketchup model and Enscape was used to render and export the scene to 360 photos. For exterior scenes, Sketchup scenes were exported directly to Lumion where materials and textures were mapped prior to rendering the 360 photos.

Project Background

The Virginia Tech Innovation Campus and Academic Building (ICAB) is centered around principles of sustainability, accessibility, flexibility, and integrated technology. When completed, the 300,000-square-foot building will provide instruction, research, office, and support spaces for graduate-level programs in Computer Science and Computer Engineering, as well as select other programs.

I was brought in during the construction documentation phase to model, render, integrate and deploy VR experiences. The final product was used to create an immersive experience for a client's fundraiser and to enhance the client's understanding of the design.
